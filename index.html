MAARS Workshop

As modern robotics systems move out of controlled environments into less structured scenarios, it becomes crucial to address the possibility of adversarial interventions. In addition to tasks with an expected, known adversary, such as search and evasion, cyber security, or opposed games, this also includes tasks such as autonomous driving which have extreme safety critical risks when faced with bad actors. This motivates the study of resilient and verified autonomous behaviors with respect to realistic adversarial model classes. Adversarial games are an important framework for performing research in this domain. This workshop focuses on learned and formal strategy in multi-agent adversarial games. We aim to make connections between leading researchers in subdomains including multi-agent systems, reinforcement learning, control theory, and formal methods to drive forward interdisciplinary research in adversarial resilience and strategy.
